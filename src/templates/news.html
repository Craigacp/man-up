Hack-a-thon 0: Cantina Game

foxxy, 13/02/2011

Create a game for 4 to 8 players to be played at a table in a noisy bar.

The games will be judged in The Student Union bar on the evening of Saturday 26th February after the hack-a-thon, which will take place in the Kilburn building between 10am and 6pm, you will need an out of hours pass. There will be six groups of judgeis and your game will be played by each one for ten minutes at a time. Members of your team will be allowed to join in the games in order to ensure the judges play them correctly and to provide tech support. You will have 2 minutes to explain the rules to the judges before the round starts.

Your game may use any hardware, software or language. There are plugs and free wifi within the bar.

The games with be judged on the following criteria.

  • Entertainment (out of 5)
    1. Didn't want to play it at all
    2. Wanted to stop playing after 1 minute
    3. Wanted to stop playing after 5 minutes
    4. Wanted to stop playing after 10 minutes
    5. Wanted to keep playing
  • Coolness (out of 5)
    1. Very embarrassing to play, got some funny looks
    2. Slightly embarrassing to play
    3. Not embarrassing but didn't interest anyone else in the bar
    4. Cool, others looked on in interest
    5. Very cool, other people wanted to join in
  • Ease of Use (out of 5)
    1. Game crashed
    2. Spent most of the time trying to understand the game
    3. Spent some of the time trying to understand the game
    4. Didn't really have to think about how to play
    5. Didn't have to think at all about the how it worked, just got on with the playing.

A maximum of six teams may take part and we'll operate on a first come first serve basis. Register your team at Thursday's meeting. The is no limit to the number of people you may have in your team. You need to the think of a team name.

Judging will take place between 9pm and 11pm at The Student Union bar. The winning team receives the special hack-a-thon badges and a Reality Mug. Assuming we have 6 teams, the schedule for the evening is as follows.

  • 8:30pm Arrive at The Student Union with your game
  • 9:00pm Round 1
  • 9:15pm Round 2
  • 9:30pm Break
  • 9:40pm Round 3
  • 9:55pm Round 4
  • 10:10pm Break
  • 10:20pm Round 5
  • 10:35pm Round 6
  • 11:00pm Winners announcement and awards

It is recommended that you start creating you entry straight away. At the very least, you should have designed the game before the hack-a-thon so that all you need to do is the coding.

We are aware that for many this will be their first hack-a-thon and probably the first time you've created something outside of the course. Do not be scared, give it a go and you'll get computer hacking skills. eldog and foxxy will be available throughout the hack-a-thon to assist people where they can. Come to the master class on Wednesday, foxxy will show you some great tools that you might want to use. We'll also talk to anyone after Thursday's meeting that wants advice on how to implement their idea.

FREE BEER - MAN-UP starts Feb 10th 5:00pm @ LF15, Kilburn Building

eldog, 04/02/2011

You are invited to the premier meeting of MANchester Ultimate Programming; a society that uses computer science to create things that are cool yet well implemented.



One of the reasons we are starting this society is because there is a most unfortunate lack of actual computing clubs within the University of Manchester. Labs and examinations are considered the be all and end all of computer science.

This is unacceptable, we are supposed to be some of the brightest minds in the country, we should expect more from ourselves especially in a field full of such tremendous power and potential. If we don’t bother trying we will most likely become useless technically-deluded banes of the IT industry, writing broken bloatware to clog up the next edition of Windows and further securing computers in the minds of the layman as Excel sheets and word documents.

This society aims change the belief that “computer science is boring” and allow people to be part of a community where they can share their creative ideas, push their abilities and explore the limits of what is sanely possible.

MAN-UP for dummies

Step 0: come to meetings


Step 1: make cool things


Step 2: get badges


It is of course a little most sophisticated than that. The inner workings of the society and details on forthcoming events will all be explained at the first meeting. Everyone is welcome, undergraduates, postgraduates, anyone.

What do I do now?

Come to room LF15 in the Kilburn building at 5pm on Thurday February 10th for the first meeting and start getting involved.

Also, if it helps you make your decision - there’s going to be free beer.

If you have any burning questions and want to find out more feel free to follow these instructions to get yourself a handle and on to the mailing list where you can join in discussions.

  1. You must have a Google Account. If not, get one
  2. Click "login" in the top right. This will take to Google's log in page. Once there, do the only thing you can do and log in. This will return you to MAN-UP
  3. You'll now see a long number in the top right. This is your Google account ID and, at the moment, your handle. Click the number to change your handle.
  4. Pick a cool handle. Don't pick a lame one, you'll be stuck with it forever. Actually, you can change it but just get it right the first time then you won’t have to bother.
  5. Right, now join the mailing list/Google group. When you join the mailing list, set your nickname to be the same as your handle.
