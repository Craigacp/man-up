Hack-a-thon 0: Premise

Create a game for 4 to 8 players to be played at a table in a noisy bar.

The games will be judged in The Student Union bar on the evening of Saturday 26th February after the hack-a-thon, which will take place in the Kilburn building between 9:30am and 7:30pm, you will need an out of hours pass. The judges will play your game for ten minutes per round, and there will be as many rounds as there are teams. Members of your team will be allowed to join in the games in order to ensure the judges play them correctly and to provide tech support. You will have 2 minutes to explain the rules to the judges before the round starts.

Your game may use any hardware, software or language. There are plugs and free wifi within the bar.

The games with be judged on the following criteria.

  • Entertainment (out of 5)
    1. Didn't want to play it at all
    2. Wanted to stop playing after 1 minute
    3. Wanted to stop playing after 5 minutes
    4. Wanted to stop playing after 10 minutes
    5. Wanted to keep playing
  • Coolness (out of 5)
    1. Very embarrassing to play, got some funny looks
    2. Slightly embarrassing to play
    3. Not embarrassing but didn't interest anyone else in the bar
    4. Cool, others looked on in interest
    5. Very cool, other people wanted to join in
  • Ease of Use (out of 5)
    1. Game crashed
    2. Spent most of the time trying to understand the game
    3. Spent some of the time trying to understand the game
    4. Didn't really have to think about how to play
    5. Didn't have to think at all about the how it worked, just got on with the playing.

A maximum of six teams may take part and we'll operate on a first come first serve basis. Register your team at Thursday's meeting. The is no limit to the number of people you may have in your team. You need to the think of a team name.

Judging will take place between 9pm and 11pm at The Student Union bar. The winning team receives the special hack-a-thon badges and a Reality Mug. Assuming we have 6 teams, the schedule for the evening is as follows.

  • 8:30pm Arrive at The Student Union with your game
  • 9:00pm Round 1
  • 9:15pm Round 2
  • 9:30pm Break
  • 9:40pm Round 3
  • 9:55pm Round 4
  • 10:10pm Break
  • 10:20pm Round 5
  • 10:35pm Round 6
  • 11:00pm Winners announcement and awards

It is recommended that you start creating you entry straight away. At the very least, you should have designed the game before the hack-a-thon so that all you need to do is the coding.

We are aware that for many this will be their first hack-a-thon and probably the first time you've created something outside of the course. Do not be scared, give it a go and you'll get computer hacking skills. eldog and foxxy will be available throughout the hack-a-thon to assist people where they can. Come to the master class on Wednesday, foxxy will show you some great tools that you might want to use. We'll also talk to anyone after Thursday's meeting that wants advice on how to implement their idea.
